COVID-19 Deaths and Race

Let's take a closer look...

The bar chart below presents the data from the previous slide to allow side by side comparisons for each state. The first bar in each pair represents the percentage of total deaths for that race, while the second bar in each pair represents the percentage of total population for that race.

In the United States overall, 22.6% of all COVID-19 deaths are Black, while they only make up 12.5% of the population. Looking closer at the states discovered in the previous page, it is revealed that Michigan (27.8%), District of Columbia (27.3%), Missouri (22.8), Louisiana (20%), and South Carolina (18.9%) have the largest disproportionate number of COVID-19 deaths for Blacks. Additionally, New Mexico (50.9%) has the largest disproportionate number of COVID-19 deaths overall and for American Indians/Alaska Natives.
